**Layer 1: Decision Guidance** (~70-90 lines):

1. **When to Apply Tension:**
   - ALWAYS at PEAK beats (mandatory for all archetypes)
   - Archetype-driven frequency table:
     | Archetype Group | Tension Frequency | Sections with Tension |
     | HIGH tension: Brutalist, Neubrutalism, Kinetic, Neon Noir | Most sections (3-5 per page) |
     | MEDIUM tension: Editorial, Vaporwave, Playful/Startup, AI-Native, Dark Academia | 2-3 per page |
     | LOW tension: Swiss, Japanese Minimal, Warm Artisan, Luxury, Neo-Corporate | 1-2 per page (PEAK only) |
     | MODERATE tension: Ethereal, Organic, Retro-Future, Glassmorphism, Data-Dense | 1-3 per page |

2. **5 Tension Levels -- Safe vs Aggressive Ranges:**
   Each level has a SAFE implementation (smaller deviation) and AGGRESSIVE implementation (extreme deviation). Archetype determines which range to use.

   | Level | Safe Range | Aggressive Range | Archetype Default |
   | 1. Scale Violence | 2-3x scale deviation | 5-10x scale deviation | HIGH archetypes -> aggressive, LOW -> safe |
   | 2. Material Collision | Contrasting textures side by side | Clashing materials (glass on concrete, neon on paper) | By archetype personality |
   | 3. Temporal Disruption | Slightly mismatched timing | Dramatically delayed/accelerated elements | By archetype motion intensity |
   | 4. Dimensional Break | Subtle depth shift (shadow, parallax) | Full 3D break, perspective explosion | By archetype tech comfort |
   | 5. Interaction Shock | Unexpected hover response | Dramatic state change on interaction | By archetype playfulness |

3. **Dual Adjacency Rules:**
   - Rule 1: If two sections both have tension, they MUST use different tension levels (no two adjacent Scale Violence sections)
   - Rule 2: At least 1 non-tension section must separate any two tension sections (exception: HIGH-tension archetypes where 2 adjacent tension sections are allowed IF different levels)
   - These rules ensure tension moments feel like deliberate punctuation, not visual noise

4. **Boldness Calibration:**
   - Default to the AGGRESSIVE range, not the safe range. Safe is the fallback, not the default.
   - "If the tension doesn't make you slightly uncomfortable, it's not tension -- it's decoration"
   - The creative director reviews tension moments and pushes further, never pulls back
   - Tension is ALWAYS intentional: it must have a clear purpose (draw attention to CTA, create emotional impact, break monotony)

**Layer 3: Integration Context** (~40-50 lines):
- **-> Emotional Arc**: PEAK beats MUST have tension. TENSION beats exist specifically for tension moments. Other beats can have tension based on archetype frequency.
- **-> Design DNA**: All tension TSX uses DNA tokens. The `--color-tension` token exists specifically for tension moments. Forbidden patterns from DNA override tension (e.g., if DNA forbids gradients, no gradient-based tension).
- **-> Cinematic Motion**: Tension moments often override normal motion patterns. TENSION beat scroll behavior is "continuous" regardless of the beat's default.
- **-> Anti-Slop Gate**: Creative Courage category (5 points) rewards tension. A page with NO tension moments fails Creative Courage unless the archetype is explicitly minimal.
- **-> MASTER-PLAN.md**: Tension types and positions are pre-assigned in the master plan. Builders execute the assigned tension, not choose their own.

**Layer 4: Anti-Patterns** (~40-50 lines):
- **Anti-Pattern: Safe Tension** -- Implementing tension at Level 1 (Scale Violence) with only 1.5x scaling. Why bad: that's not tension, it's a mild emphasis. Instead: minimum 2x for safe range, 5x+ for aggressive.
- **Anti-Pattern: Tension Everywhere** -- Every section has a tension moment. Why bad: if everything is surprising, nothing is. Instead: follow archetype frequency table and adjacency rules.
- **Anti-Pattern: Clashing Tensions** -- Two adjacent sections both using Scale Violence. Why bad: monotonous, feels accidental. Instead: different tension levels per adjacency rules.
- **Anti-Pattern: Arbitrary Colors in Tension** -- Using hex values like `#ff0000` for tension elements. Why bad: breaks DNA token system. Instead: use `var(--color-tension)`, `var(--color-accent)`.
- **Anti-Pattern: Tension Without Purpose** -- Adding visual shock that doesn't serve the page's story. Why bad: feels gimmicky. Instead: every tension moment must either draw attention to a key element or create an emotional beat.
- **Anti-Pattern: Pulling Back** -- Implementing the aggressive range but then softening it because "it looks too bold." Why bad: the whole point of tension is to be bold. Instead: if it feels too bold, that's the right amount. The creative director pushes further, never pulls back.
