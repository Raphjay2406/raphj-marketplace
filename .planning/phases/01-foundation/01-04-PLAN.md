**Layer 1: Decision Guidance** (~50-60 lines):

Opening: The Anti-Slop Gate is a POST-REVIEW quality enforcement system. It does NOT run inline during building. Builders focus on building; the gate runs during `/gen:verify` via the quality-reviewer agent.

**When the Gate Runs:**
- After ALL sections in a wave are built
- During `/gen:verify` command
- Quality-reviewer agent executes the scoring protocol
- Results are presented to the user with full transparency

**How Scoring Works:**
- 7 categories with WEIGHTED point distribution (not flat 5 per category)
- Design quality categories (Typography, Depth & Polish) weighted highest
- Total: 35 points across all categories
- Penalties applied AFTER the 35-point total for specific violations
- Final score = 35-point total minus penalties

**Named Quality Tiers:**
| Tier | Score Range | Meaning | Action |
|------|------------|---------|--------|
| Honoree-Level | 33-35 | Exceptional -- Awwwards Honoree territory | Ship with pride |
| SOTD-Ready | 30-32 | Site of the Day competitive | Ship confidently |
| Strong | 28-29 | Solid premium quality | Ship, minor polish recommended |
| Pass | 25-27 | Meets minimum quality bar | Ship, but review improvement areas |
| FAIL | <25 | Below quality standard | Mandatory rework |

**Decision Tree:**
- Score >= 25: PASS (proceed to next phase or ship)
- Score < 25: FAIL (generate remediation tasks, re-execute affected sections)
- Score >= 30 with penalty deductions below 25: FAIL (penalties can push a pass to fail)

**Layer 2: Award-Winning Examples** (~180-220 lines):

This is the core of the skill -- the full 35-point scoring breakdown.

**Category 1: Colors (5 points)**
| # | Check | Points | How to Verify |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|
| C1 | Uses DNA color tokens exclusively (no arbitrary hex/rgb) | 2 | Grep for hex values not in DNA palette |
| C2 | Primary/secondary/accent all visible and distinct | 1 | Visual check: can you identify 3 distinct accent colors? |
| C3 | Expressive tokens used (glow, tension, highlight, or signature) | 1 | Grep for --color-glow, --color-tension, --color-highlight, --color-signature |
| C4 | Color contrast meets WCAG AA (4.5:1 text, 3:1 large text) | 1 | Automated contrast checker |

**Category 2: Typography (6 points) -- DESIGN QUALITY (weighted highest)**
| # | Check | Points | How to Verify |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|
| T1 | Display font is distinctive (not Inter/Roboto/system-ui for headings) | 2 | Font audit: check font-family on h1-h3 elements |
| T2 | 3+ font weights visible with clear hierarchy | 1 | Visual check: bold headings, medium subheads, regular body |
| T3 | Letter-spacing tuned (tight on headings, relaxed or wide on labels/caps) | 1 | Check tracking- classes on headings, tracking-wide on small caps |
| T4 | Line heights varied (tight on headings 1.1-1.2, relaxed on body 1.5-1.7) | 1 | Check leading- classes or line-height values |
| T5 | Typographic surprise present (gradient text, variable font, oversized heading, text-stroke, mixed serif+sans) | 1 | Visual check: at least one unexpected type treatment |

**Category 3: Layout (5 points)**
| # | Check | Points | How to Verify |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|
| L1 | No two adjacent sections share the same layout pattern | 2 | Visual comparison of consecutive sections |
| L2 | Asymmetric or dynamic composition present (not all centered/grid) | 1 | At least one section breaks symmetric default |
| L3 | Negative space used intentionally (not just padding-everything-equally) | 1 | Check for varied spacing, breathing room |
| L4 | Grid breaking moment exists (element overlapping grid, bleeding to edge) | 1 | Visual check for at least one grid-breaking element |

**Category 4: Depth & Polish (6 points) -- DESIGN QUALITY (weighted highest)**
| # | Check | Points | How to Verify |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|
| D1 | Shadows are layered (2-3 shadow layers, not shadow-md alone) | 2 | Grep for shadow-\[.*,.*\] or multiple shadow values |
| D2 | Borders are subtle (opacity-based or gradient, not solid gray) | 1 | Check border colors: should use DNA tokens or rgba |
| D3 | Glass/frost/blur OR texture element present | 1 | Grep for backdrop-blur or noise/grain texture |
| D4 | Corner radii varied per element type (not uniform rounded-lg everywhere) | 1 | Check: buttons vs cards vs badges have different radii |
| D5 | Micro-details present (noise texture, gradient borders, custom selection color, subtle patterns) | 1 | At least 2 micro-details visible |

**Category 5: Motion (5 points)**
| # | Check | Points | How to Verify |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|
| M1 | Entrance animations present and varied (not all fade-up) | 2 | Check: at least 2 different entrance animation types |
| M2 | Hover states are designed (not just opacity change) | 1 | Check interactive elements: scale, color shift, shadow change, etc. |
| M3 | Scroll-triggered animation present | 1 | At least one element animated on scroll |
| M4 | Animation timing uses DNA motion tokens (not hardcoded 300ms) | 1 | Grep for DNA motion CSS variables |

**Category 6: Creative Courage (5 points)**
| # | Check | Points | How to Verify |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|
| CC1 | Signature element is present and prominent | 2 | The DNA-defined signature element is visible |
| CC2 | At least one "wow moment" that would make a user pause | 1 | Subjective: would you screenshot this to share? |
| CC3 | Creative tension moment present (intentional rule break) | 1 | Tension override documented in SUMMARY.md |
| CC4 | Something unexpected -- element that defies generic patterns | 1 | Not a standard card/grid/hero layout |

**Category 7: UX Intelligence (3 points)**
| # | Check | Points | How to Verify |
|---|-------|--------|---------------|
| U1 | Navigation has current-page indicator with distinct styling | 1 | Active nav item is visually differentiated |
| U2 | Interactive elements give feedback under 100ms | 1 | Click/hover response is immediate |
| U3 | CTA hierarchy clear, no generic text ("Submit", "Learn More", "Click Here") | 1 | Check all button/link text for generics |

**Penalty System:**
| Violation | Penalty | Condition | Detection |
|-----------|---------|-----------|-----------|

| No creative tension | -5 | Zero tension moments on entire page | Check SUMMARY.md for tension documentation |
| Generic CTA text | -2 | Per instance of "Submit"/"Learn More"/"Click Here"/"Get Started" (max -6) | Text grep |

**Layer 3: Integration Context** (~40-50 lines):
- **Gate -> Quality Reviewer**: The quality-reviewer agent runs this protocol during `/gen:verify`. It scores each category, applies penalties, outputs the scoring template.
- **Gate -> DNA**: C1 check verifies DNA token compliance. M4 verifies motion token usage. CC1 verifies signature element.
- **Gate -> Archetypes**: Penalty for forbidden patterns uses archetype-specific forbidden list.
- **Gate -> Emotional Arc**: Layout diversity (L1) connects to beat-based section differentiation.
- **Gate -> Awwwards 4-Axis**: Gate and Awwwards are SEPARATE systems. Gate = enforcement (pass/fail). Awwwards = aspiration (quality level prediction). Both run during verify, gate first.
- **Gate is NOT a self-check**: Builders do NOT run this during building. The gate runs AFTER build, during review. This prevents context bloat and lets builders focus on craft.

**Layer 4: Anti-Patterns** (~30-40 lines):
- **Anti-Pattern: Gate as Inline Checklist** -- Running the 35-point check while building each section. Why bad: consumes 30%+ of builder's context on scoring instead of building. Instead: builders focus on craft, gate runs during separate verify phase.
- **Anti-Pattern: Gaming the Score** -- Adding gratuitous micro-details or fake "wow moments" just to pass. Why bad: the gate measures quality, not checklist completion. A forced wow moment is worse than none. Instead: design with intent, score reflects genuine quality.
- **Anti-Pattern: Penalty Ignorance** -- Scoring 30/35 but losing 8 in penalties for forbidden patterns. Why bad: penalties exist because those violations destroy archetype integrity. Instead: check forbidden patterns BEFORE review using DNA compliance hook.
- **Anti-Pattern: Flat Remediation** -- Fixing all failed checks with minimum effort. Why bad: minimum effort produces minimum quality. Instead: each remediation should be a genuine improvement, not a checkbox exercise.

**Target length: 400-550 lines.** This is a Core tier skill -- the quality enforcement backbone.
