**Trigger and Role** (~5 lines):
Trigger: during brainstorm when generating creative directions, when needing unexpected visual language, when breaking industry conventions.
Role: "You are a cross-pollination strategist who creates unexpected visual language by borrowing design principles from deliberately distant industries, with structured coherence guardrails."

**Layer 1: Decision Guidance** (~60-80 lines):
- When to use: During brainstorm after initial research, before generating creative directions
- When NOT to use: During build execution (cross-pollination decisions are locked into DNA by then)
- Decision tree: "Is the project in a saturated visual space? -> Yes -> aggressive cross-pollination (2-3 breaks). No -> light pollination (1 break, closer industries)"
- Boldness calibration table linking archetype personality to number of constraint breaks:

| Archetype Category | Break Count | Cross-Pollination Distance |
|Conservative (Swiss, Neo-Corporate, Japanese Minimal) | 1 | Adjacent or moderate |
|Moderate (Editorial, Organic, Warm Artisan, Data-Dense, Glassmorphism) | 1-2 | Moderate to distant |
|Bold (Brutalist, Kinetic, Neon Noir, Vaporwave, Retro-Future, Playful) | 2-3 | Deliberately distant |
|Luxury/Ethereal | 1 (but high refinement) | Curated, specific |

**Layer 2: Industry Reference Library** (~200-250 lines):
Cover 12 industry verticals. For EACH vertical, provide:

1. **Industry name and description** (1 line)
2. **6-8 exemplary sites** described as PATTERNS, not just URLs. Format: "Site Name demonstrates [specific pattern]: [2-sentence description of what they do and why it works]." Focus on patterns that survive site redesigns.
3. **4-6 industry conventions/rules** structured for breaking. Format: "RULE: [convention]. WHY it exists: [rationale]. WHEN to break: [conditions]."
4. **2-3 cross-pollination pairings** with principle-level rationale. Format: "PAIR WITH: [distant industry]. BORROW: [design principle, not surface trait]. MANIFESTS AS: [visual/structural implementation]. DNA COMPATIBILITY: [how it maps to tokens]."

The 12 verticals (matching research recommendation):
1. SaaS / Developer Tools
2. E-commerce / Fashion
3. E-commerce / General Retail
4. Creative Agency / Design Studio
5. Portfolio / Personal
6. Blog / Publication / Media
7. Health / Wellness
8. Food / Beverage / Restaurant
9. Fintech / Dashboard
10. Architecture / Interior Design
11. Education / Social Impact
12. Gaming / Entertainment

CRITICAL QUALITY RULES:
- Cross-pollination pairings MUST be at the PRINCIPLE level, not surface level. BAD: "borrow serifs from fashion." GOOD: "Borrow fashion's editorial pacing principle: information reveals through scroll-driven narrative chapters rather than feature grids. Manifests as: full-viewport image-text interleaves with dramatic whitespace between sections."
- Industry rules MUST be real conventions that practitioners would recognize, not generic observations
- Reference sites MUST describe observable patterns, not just names. If the site redesigns, the pattern description should still be useful
- Each vertical should feel like it was written by someone who actually studies that industry's design landscape

**Layer 3: Integration Context** (~40-50 lines):
- **Constraint-Breaking Protocol**: The structured format for presenting breaks to users:
  ```
  INDUSTRY RULE: [convention]
  WE BREAK THIS BY: [alternative approach]
  BORROWED FROM: [source industry + principle]
  WHY: [rationale tied to project goals]
  DNA EXPRESSION: [which DNA tokens carry this break]
  ARCHETYPE CHECK: [compatible/incompatible with chosen archetype]
  ```
- **Double Coherence Guardrail**: Every borrowed element and every break must pass two gates:
  1. DNA Token Gate: Can this be expressed using the project's 12 color tokens, type scale, spacing scale, and motion language? If it requires tokens outside the DNA system, it fails.
  2. Archetype Compatibility Gate: Does this conflict with the chosen archetype's forbidden patterns? If the archetype forbids gradients and the borrowed element requires gradients, it fails.
- **How design-brainstorm references this skill**: The brainstorm protocol calls this skill during direction generation. For each of the 3 creative directions, brainstorm pulls: 1-2 constraint breaks from the relevant industry's rule catalog, 1 cross-pollination pairing, and runs both coherence gates.
- **Integration with creative-tension skill**: Cross-pollination breaks can serve as the creative tension moment for a direction. Map borrowed principles to the 5 tension levels (Scale Violence, Material Collision, Temporal Disruption, Dimensional Break, Interaction Shock).

**Layer 4: Anti-Patterns** (~40-50 lines):
5 anti-patterns, each with Name, What Goes Wrong, Instead:

1. **Surface-Level Borrowing**: Borrowing "serifs" or "dark mode" from another industry. Instead: borrow the underlying design PRINCIPLE (editorial pacing, information density philosophy, interaction model).

2. **Kitchen Sink Pollination**: Borrowing from 4+ industries in one direction, creating incoherence. Instead: 1 primary distant-industry pairing per direction, maximum 2 borrowed principles.

3. **Breaking Without Rebuilding**: Stating a rule is broken but not providing the replacement approach. Instead: every break must include the alternative and how it maps to DNA tokens.

4. **Ignoring Coherence Gates**: Borrowing an element that can't be expressed in DNA tokens or conflicts with the archetype. Instead: always run both gates before presenting a break.

5. **Safe Adjacent Borrowing**: "SaaS borrows from other SaaS" or "fashion borrows from luxury." Instead: force at least one pairing from a genuinely distant industry (3+ steps removed in the pairing matrix).
